## Introduction

Choosing the right headphones can feel overwhelming with so many options on the market. In this guide, we'll help you understand the key factors for making the best decision.

## Types of Headphones

### Over-ear (Circumaural)

Over-ear headphones fully cover your ears, offering:

- **Better sound quality** thanks to larger drivers
- **Superior comfort** for long sessions
- **Better isolation** from outside noise

Ideal for home or office use.

### On-ear (Supra-aural)

On-ear headphones rest on your ears:

- **More portable** than over-ear
- **Less heat** on your ears
- **Generally lower price**

Perfect for those seeking balance between portability and quality.

### In-ear (Earbuds)

In-ear headphones go inside the ear canal:

- **Maximum portability**
- **Ideal for sports**
- **Good passive isolation**

The preferred choice for use on the go.

## Important Features

### Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)

ANC technology uses microphones to detect ambient noise and generate sound waves that counteract it. Especially useful for:

- Airplane travel
- Noisy offices
- Public transportation

### Connectivity

- **Bluetooth**: Freedom of movement, but battery-dependent
- **Wired**: No latency, lossless quality
- **USB-C**: Increasingly common in smartphones

### Battery Life

For wireless headphones, look for at least:

- 20 hours for over-ear
- 6-8 hours for in-ear (more with the case)

## Price Ranges

### Entry-level ($50-100)

Good for casual use. Brands like JBL, Sony (basic range), and Audio-Technica offer solid options.

### Mid-range ($100-250)

Here you'll find excellent value. Notable options include Sony WH-1000XM4, Bose QC45.

### Premium ($250+)

For audiophiles and professionals. Sony WH-1000XM5, Apple AirPods Max, Sennheiser Momentum.

## Conclusion

The best choice depends on your primary use, budget, and personal preferences. We recommend trying several models before deciding, paying attention to comfort and the sound profile you enjoy most.
